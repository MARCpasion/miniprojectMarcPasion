Laoag History

Before the arrival of the Spaniards, the region of Ilocos Norte, Ilocos Sur, Abra, and La Union was renowned for its gold mines and trade with Japan and China. The Austronesian inhabitants called their place samtoy, meaning "this is our language." In 1571, Spanish conquistadors landed in Vigan and proceeded towards Laoag, Currimao, and Badoc, where they discovered numerous sheltered coves where locals lived in harmony. The region was named "Ylocos" and its people "Ylocanos." As Christianization grew, the landscape of the area was transformed, with vast tracts of land used for churches and bell towers. However, the Spanish colonization was never completely successful, leading to several uprisings against the colonizers. In 1818, a Spanish Royal Decree was promulgated dividing the Province of Ilocos Norte from Ilocos Sur, and Laoag City was made the capital of Ilocos Norte. Despite being converted into a city in 1965, it remained the capital of Ilocos Norte.
